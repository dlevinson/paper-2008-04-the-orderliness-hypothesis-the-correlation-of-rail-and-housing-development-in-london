# The Orderliness Hypothesis: The Correlation Of Rail And Housing Development In London

This folder is a cleaned public-upload package for `paper-2008-04`.

## Citation

David M. Levinson. (2008). The orderliness hypothesis: The correlation of rail and housing development in London. Journal of Transport History, 29(1), 98-114. https://doi.org/10.7227/TJTH.29.1.8

## Paper Evidence

The inspected published PDF says the paper measures correlations between station-density rank and population-density rank for London administrative districts. It uses Underground opening/closing dates from Rose, surface rail dates from Borley, station placement in current boroughs, and population data for 33 current boroughs from the 1801 census onward after recoding to current areas.

## Package Boundary

This package keeps the paper-specific correlation workbooks and supporting workbooks. The broader shared London population/network/GIS inputs are not duplicated here; they are referenced through `documentation/SHARED_DATA_POINTERS.md` and the cleaned `paper-2008-03` London coevolution package.

## Contents

- `paper/`: local reference copy of the published PDF for audit convenience.
- `data/orderliness_workbooks/`: correlation, rank, workplace-population, railway-bills, subdivision, and London trend workbooks.
- `documentation/`: source manifest, workbook overview, shared data pointer, and explicit exclusions.

## Exclusions

Drafts, referee/review material, submission instructions, figure image/PDF outputs, Pages bundles, source-document PDFs, compressed duplicates, and shared London inputs are excluded from this package. See `documentation/EXCLUDED_NONARCHIVAL_FILES.csv`.
